The Hard Truth About Adult CPAP

Sleepnet is spending its largest R&D line item fighting an unwinnable war

Sleepnet currently allocates approximately $350,000/year in R&D to adult CPAP masks — more than any other product line. Meanwhile, ResMed holds 62% of the global CPAP market. Philips Respironics and Fisher & Paykel Healthcare control most of the rest. Sleepnet's estimated share: ~2%.

Every R&D dollar spent on adult CPAP competes against companies with 200× Sleepnet's revenue, established hospital ecosystems, and multi-billion-dollar marketing budgets. That same dollar invested where Sleepnet has a real edge — pediatric CPAP, industrial respirators, or entirely new verticals — has dramatically higher expected return.

But Sleepnet has genuine competitive advantages — in the right markets

Proprietary AIR°gel Technology — A patented medical-grade silicone gel cushion that no competitor has replicated. Provides measurable comfort, pressure distribution, and seal quality advantages across product lines.

35 Years of Manufacturing Expertise — FDA-compliant cleanroom, NIOSH N95/N99 certification, ISO compliance, precision gel molding capabilities. Infrastructure that takes competitors years to build.

100% American-Made — In an era of reshoring, supply chain concerns, and Buy American Act procurement preferences, Sleepnet's Hampton NH manufacturing is a genuine competitive asset.

International Distribution to 53+ Countries — Named NH 2023 Exporter of the Year. Existing global reach that most companies Sleepnet's size don't have.

The Strategic Question

Sleepnet doesn't need to become a bigger company to grow. It needs to become a sharper one — investing where it has a real edge and stopping investment where it doesn't. This assessment lays out exactly where those edges are, backed by market data, competitive analysis, and four specific new revenue verticals.

The Big Picture for Ronnie

Sleepnet's AIR°gel technology and precision molding infrastructure are platform capabilities, not product-specific ones. The same gel that seals a CPAP mask to a patient's face can seal a ski goggle, cushion a fighter pilot's oxygen mask, line an LED beauty device, pad an earbud, or protect a firefighter. Eight new markets. Same factory. Same core IP. The question isn't whether Sleepnet can do this — it's which markets to attack first.
